= Learn about the NetApp AI Data Engine
:icons: font
:imagesdir: ../media/

[.lead]
NetApp AI Data Engine (AIDE) is an advanced computing system you can optionally install and integrate with your AFX cluster. It extends AFX by providing a powerful GPU-based processing capability to support your artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) applications. AIDE enables you to enhance the value and visibility of your data while retaining all the benefits of ONTAP.

== AIDE overview

AIDE is a system designed to accelerate and simplify the preparation and management of your data for AI and ML workloads. It integrates tightly with the NetApp AFX storage system, leveraging the high-performance and scalable AFX storage foundation while adding specialized services for AI data processing, governance, and workflow automation.

A core strength of AIDE is its ability to centralize and automate the management of metadata across large, distributed datasets. It can continuously catalog and update its metadata store based on the data in your ONTAP volumes, enabling users to search, classify, and apply governance policies efficiently. The internal processing pipeline creates AI-ready datasets, including the requisite generation of vector embeddings for semantic search and retrieval tasks.

Security and governance are also central to its design. AIDE provides advanced authentication, robust access controls, continuous data classification, and policy-driven enforcement for the protection of sensitive information. Features such as automated redaction, masking, and audit logging help organizations meet the applicable regulatory requirements and protect critical data throughout the entire AI lifecycle.

AIDE empowers data engineers and scientists by providing tools for rapid data discovery, curation, and workflow management. By using dedicated AIDE console and enhanced AFX REST API, users can create curated data collections, perform vector searches, and integrate with external AI and GenAI applications. AIDE reduces the manual effort needed and enables teams to focus on extracting insights from their data.

== Installation options

There are two primary options available for installing and integrating AIDE with your AIDE cluster.

=== Setup AFX and AIDE together

If you're creating a new AI data infrastructure, installing AIDE together with the AFX storage system provides a streamlined and unified deployment experience. AIDE and AFX are installed and configured together as part of a single setup workflow. This ensures all the components are optimally integrated from the beginning, with the AFX storage and AI data services aligned and available for immediate use.

Deploying AIDE and AFX together offers administrators several benefits, including automated configuration steps, pre-validated compatibility, and a simplified onboarding process. The entire system is immediately available for AI workloads as soon as installation is completed. This option is ideal for greenfield environments or organizations seeking to accelerate time-to-value with a turnkey AI data platform.

=== Integrate AIDE with an existing AFX cluster

For organizations that have already installed and use an AFX storage cluster, AIDE can be added at any time to enhance the access and understanding of your data. In this case, AIDE is essentially deployed as an add-on to the existing AFX environment, connecting to the established cluster storage network without disrupting current operations. The integration process automatically discovers available ONTAP volumes and synchronizes metadata.

This is a flexible approach allowing organizations to adopt AI data services at their own pace. The AIDE automation, governance, and data curation tools are layered onto the current AFX storage infrastructure. It’s an excellent choice for companies looking to upgrade and modernize their data workflows by expanding into the provided AI and ML capabilities without needing to alter their storage foundation.
